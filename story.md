# The Captive's Escape (3)

You awaken in a dimly lit cell, the air thick with the scent of damp stone and decay. The walls are cold against your skin, and the floor is covered in a layer of dust and mold. Sparse light filters in through a small, high window, barely enough to see the crude wooden door at the far end of the cell. The only sound is the distant drip of water, a constant reminder of your isolation. You have no idea how you got here, or where 'here' is, but one thing is certain: you must find a way out before your captors return.

> **You chose:** Inspect the cell for any weaknesses or clues about your location, like markings or carvings on the walls or floor.

---

As you stand, you begin to inspect the cell meticulously. The walls are thick and solid, with no visible cracks or weaknesses that might allow an escape. However, as you run your fingers along the stone, you notice faint scratch marks near the floor. They look like they were made by something sharp, perhaps a nail or a piece of metal. Following the marks upward, you see they lead to a small, partially obscured carving of a key and a lock. Could this be a clue to your location or a way out?
